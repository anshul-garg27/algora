# CRIB SHEET — Hiring Manager / Behavioral (45-105 min — a REAL elimination round)

A candidate cleared every tech round and failed here. Another's HM round
turned fully technical (HLD + language internals). Prepare both modes.

## What's coming (in rough order)
1. Tell me about yourself (≤2 min, rehearsed).
2. **Past-project architecture walkthrough** — THE most-asked Uber question
   (6x last year). 5 min uninterrupted + 15 min probing: why that DB? real
   QPS? what breaks if X dies? what was YOURS vs the team's? what would you
   change today?
3. 4-5 behavioral: conflict (4x) · ownership beyond role · major decision ·
   deadlines/prioritization · mentoring/inclusion · why leave + why Uber (4x).
4. Possible technical ambush from YOUR project domain (concurrency, language
   internals). One candidate's "behavioral" round was entirely this.
5. Your questions (have 3).

## Non-negotiables
- **"I", not "we."** Vague ownership is the #1 red flag. "The team built X;
  the piece I personally designed and defend end-to-end is ___."
- **Every story ends in a NUMBER** (latency, cost, users, revenue).
- **Never fabricate** — probes go sideways into details; fabrication snaps
  (real offer-getter's exact advice).
- Conflict story: real trade-off, you brought data, you name what YOU
  conceded. "I was right and they accepted it" = fail.
- **Why Uber: TWO specifics ready** (H3/geo infra, marketplace dispatch, an
  Uber Eng blog post). Generic "scale and impact" gets the follow-up:
  "name something specific."

## STAR skeleton (keep on a sticky note)
Situation (2 lines, stakes) → Task (your charter) → Action (3-5 steps, all
"I") → Result (number + lesson).

## Situational prompts — judgment framework, not scripts
Clarify impact → name 2-3 options → pick one WITH the reason → state the
guardrail. (Ship-or-slip bug, slacking teammate, risky PM hack, disagreeing
with manager.)

## Your 3 questions
"What separates an L4 promoted in 18 months from one who stalls?" ·
"Team's biggest technical bet this year?" · "What does on-call/incident
culture look like here?"

## Before the call
Work Document open (`../behavioral/WORK_DOCUMENT_TEMPLATE.md` — FILLED) ·
architecture diagram of your project drawable in 60s · two why-Uber
specifics memorized.
